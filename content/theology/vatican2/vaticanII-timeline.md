## Vatican II Timeline

From: [Vatican II at 60](https://www.denvercatholic.org/vatican-ii-at-60-a-timeline-of-key-moments)

> [!tip] 1959--The "Good Pope" calls a council
> The story of Vatican II began in the middle of the century with a man commonly known as _Il Papa Bueno._ On Jan. 25, Pope John XXIII called for an ecumenical council. Over the next few months, he established a preparatory commission to consult bishops to work out the council’s agenda.

> [!tip] 1960--Eleven commissions
> By *motu proprio*, Pope John XXIII established eleven commissions. Their task was to produce drafts for the coming gathering in Rome.

> [!tip] 1961--The council is called to Rome
> The council was formally summoned: It was by the apostolic constitution [_Humanae Salutis_](https://www.vatican.va/content/john-xxiii/la/apost_constitutions/1961/documents/hf_j-xxiii_apc_19611225_humanae-salutis.html) that the pope called eligible participants to Rome. Almost 2,500 bishops ultimately attended as council fathers, joined by several hundred auditors and advisers.

> [!tip] 1962--The first sessio
> The first session of the council met from Oct. 11 to Dec. 8, and work began in 16 commissions on the [16 documents](https://www.ewtn.com/catholicism/library/16-documents-of-the-second-vatican-council-1509) promulgated as a result of the Second Vatican Council. These documents cover many topics, including the collegiality of bishops, the relationship with other religions and religious freedom, as well as a number of key themes in four documents called **constitutions**.

> [!tip] 1963--A new pope
> On June 3, Pope John XXIII died at age 81. Within three weeks, a successor, Pope Paul VI, was elected. The new pontiff promptly announced that the council would continue. The second session gathered in Rome from Sept. 29 to Dec. 4.  Before it began, Pope Paul VI announced a reform of the Roman Curia. Issued on Dec. 4, _Sacrosanctum concilium_, the [**Constitution on the Sacred Liturgy**](https://www.vatican.va/archive/hist_councils/ii_vatican_council/documents/vat-ii_const_19631204_sacrosanctum-concilium_en.html), called for the use of Latin “to be preserved in the Latin rites” but also allowed for the use of local — vernacular — languages in some areas.

> [!tip] 1964--The Light of the Nations
> From Sept. 14 to Nov. 21, the council fathers gathered to continue their work. On the final day of the year’s session, [_Lumen gentium_](https://www.vatican.va/archive/hist_councils/ii_vatican_council/documents/vat-ii_const_19641121_lumen-gentium_en.html), the **Dogmatic Constitution on the Church**, was issued, among other documents. This text on the “Light of the Nations” proclaims that the laity “carry out for their own part the mission of the whole Christian people in the Church and in the world.” Also on the final day, Nov. 21, Pope Paul VI announced that the [Eucharistic fast](https://www.ewtn.com/catholicism/library/eucharistic-fasting-preparation-and-penance-1122) would be reduced to one hour.

> [!tip] A solemn end
> The fourth and final session of the council met from Sept. 14 to Dec. 8. During this session, several key documents were issued, including two more constitutions. The **Dogmatic Constitution on Divine Revelation**, [_Dei verbum_](https://www.vatican.va/archive/hist_councils/ii_vatican_council/documents/vat-ii_const_19651118_dei-verbum_en.html), asserts that “sacred tradition and Sacred Scripture … flowing from the same divine wellspring, in a certain way merge into a unity and tend toward the same end.” The **Pastoral Constitution on the Church in the Modern World**, [_Gaudium et spes_](https://www.vatican.va/archive/hist_councils/ii_vatican_council/documents/vat-ii_const_19651207_gaudium-et-spes_en.html), was the last document promulgated during the council. Mentioning the word “world” 124 times and the word “Church” 140 times, it explains that “the Church has always had the duty of scrutinizing the signs of the times and of interpreting them in the light of the Gospel” and asserts “the effort to establish a universal brotherhood is not a hopeless one.” On the final day, Dec. 8, 1965, the Second Vatican Council solemnly concluded. An extraordinary Jubilee Year was proclaimed to familiarize the faithful with the teachings of the council.
