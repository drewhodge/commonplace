_Gaudium et Spes_ 22, from the Second Vatican Council (1965), argues that human
nature, dignity, and purpose are fully revealed only through Jesus Christ, the
"Word made flesh". Often called a "Christocentric bombshell," it establishes
that Christ is the key to understanding human identity and solving modern
existential questions.
<!-- more -->

## Core Themes of GS 22

- **Christocentric Anthropology:** The paragraph shifts focus from a solely rationalistic view of humanity to one anchored in Christ. It teaches that humanity's "mystery" is only clarified in light of the incarnate Word.
- **The "Image of God" Reflected:** Christ is described as the "new Adam" who fully reveals man to himself and makes his supreme calling clear.
- **The Paschal Mystery:** Through his suffering, death, and resurrection, Christ provides a pattern for humanity, enabling humans to make their lives and deaths holy.
- **Solidarity with Humanity:** The text emphasizes that by his Incarnation, the Son of God united himself in some fashion with every human being, working with human hands and loving with a human heart.

## Key Implications

- **Human Dignity:** Human dignity is rooted in being created in God's image and redeemed by Christ.
- **Purpose of Life:** The text links human fulfillment to "a sincere gift of self" to others, mirroring Christ’s self-giving love.
- **The Church's Role:** The Church is the conduit through which this life in Christ is offered to the world, providing the "spiritual light and nourishment" necessary to navigate modern challenges. 

Pope John Paul II often highlighted this paragraph, regarding it as the essential, interpretive key to the entire document and a guide for interpreting modern existence.
