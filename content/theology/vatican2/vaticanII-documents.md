## Constitutions

These are the **foundation**, the **core documents centred on the call to holiness**.

- *Gaudium et Spes* The Church in the World
- *Sacrosanctum Concilium* Liturgy (Dogmatic Constitution)[^1]
- *Dei Verbum* Revelation (Dogmatic Constitution)[^1]
- *Lumen Gentium* The Church

## Declarations

- *Gravissimum Educationes* Declaration in Christian Education
- *Nostra Aetate* Declaration on the Relation of the Church to Non-Christian Denominations 
- *Dignitatis Humanae* Declaration in Religious Freedom

## Decrees

- *Ad Gentes* Decree on the Missionary Activity of the Church
- *Presbyterorum Ordinis* Decree on the Ministry and Life of Priests
- *Apostolicam Actuositatem* Decree on the Apostolate of the Laity
- *Optatam Totius* Decree on Priestly Training
- *Perectae Caritatis* Decree on the Adaptation and Renewal of Religious Life
- *Christus Dominus* Decree Concerning the Pastoral Office of Bishops in the Church
- *Unitatis Redintegratio* Decree on Ecumenism
- *Orientalium Ecclesiarum* Decree on the Catholic Churches of the Eastern Rite
- *Inter Mirifica* Decree on the Media of Social Communications

---

### Footnotes

[^1]: In the Catholic sense, "dogmatic" refers to truths contained in divine revelation (Scripture and Tradition) that the Church’s Magisterium has formally proclaimed as infallible, binding, and unchangeable. It signifies a divinely revealed teaching that must be believed by the faithful, such as the Trinity or the Incarnation.
